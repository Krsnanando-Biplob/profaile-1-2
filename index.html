ISKCON, or the International Society for Krishna Consciousness, is a global spiritual organization founded in 1966 by A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada. It promotes the practice of bhakti yoga (devotion) to Lord Krishna and aims to spread the teachings of ancient Vedic scriptures, particularly the Bhagavad Gita. ISKCON members engage in chanting the Hare Krishna mantra, congregational worship, and various outreach activities to foster spiritual growth and awareness. The organization has temples and centers worldwide, emphasizing community, vegetarianism, and a holistic approach to personal and societal well-being.
